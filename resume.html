Experience

ORBCOMM | Embedded Software Developer

January 2017 - April 2017 | Ottawa, ON

For my latest co-op term I worked as an embedded software developer for ORBCOMM - a big player in the Machine-to-Machine (M2M) and Internet-of-Things (IoT) space. While there, my main project was to spearhead the transition of our terminal application software from our legacy hardware to our next generation products. This involved porting Lua 5.1 code to a Lua 5.3 platform, refactoring old code, addressing outstanding tickets and implementing automated builds and testing using Jenkins.


Near the end of the term I also got a chance to work on another project. For this project my task was to implement a file upload feature which would allow for over-the-air firmware upgrades. I was working on an embedded web server running on a FRDM-K66F board. The jump to C was certainly a big one, but a much welcome change from the carefree nature of Lua.


Ravens Racing | Powertrain Team Member

January 2017 - April 2017 | Carleton University

Going to a new city meant I didn't know very many people going in. What better way to fix this than to join a student design team? Ravens Racing is a student run team at Carleton University, whose goal is to design and build a formula style race car. As part of the powertrain subteam, I dealt with the heart of the car: the engine. I took part in the tuning and maintenance of the engine, dynamometer and some of the final drive components.


I also spent many late nights in the machine shop making all sorts of parts of the car. This work involved the use of a lathe, mill, all sorts of handtools, and even welding! In May, we'll be going to Michigan in order to compete in the Formula SAE Michigan competition, where 120 teams from around the world will see who built the best car.


Waterloop | EC Brakes/Electrical Team Member

September 2016 - December 2016 | University of Waterloo

The Hyperloop is the so called fifth mode of transportation, described by Elon Musk in his 2013 white paper. Musk's SpaceX decided to host a design competion, in which teams from around the world built half-scale prototypes of their pod. The University of Waterloo's team was one of 30 teams that went to Hawthrone, California to compete.


I started off my work on the Waterloop team with the Eddy Current Braking system. I designed a bracket to hold a linear actuator within the brakes, as well as manufacturing magnet mounts. Eventually I transitioned into doing more electrical work. As an Electrical member of the team, I did lots of soldering and sourcing of parts. I also did a bit of schematic work using Eagle, which is something I want to do more of in the future.


University Health Network | Bioinformatics RA

May 2016 - August 2016 | Princess Margaret Genomics Centre

Within the University Health Network I worked for the Princess Margaret Genomics Centre. While there, I worked a lot with the MEAN (MongoDB, Express.js, AngularJS, Node.js) stack, developing applications for researchers, research groups, and lab technicians. I also deployed and maintained an OpenID Connect server, which is to be used by client applications from institutes across the country. At the end of the co-op term I recived an 'outstanding' review for my work, which is the highest review available to Waterloo co-op students.


Ryerson Rams Robotics | Team Member

July 2016 - September 2016 | Ryerson University

I found myself with some extra time after work during the co-op term, so I decided to go out and find something to do. I ended up joining this student design team, meeting with them several times a week. While there I worked with both of their teams: the VexU team, and the Mars Rover team. I did some prototyping for both teams, some testing of electrical components for the VexU team, and a lot of research of the Mars Rover team. On top of this I also participated on behalf of the club at information sessions, and helped around the shop building and fixing anything that came in.
